# I Got Married Because My Aunt Cried

There is no good reason to get married because someone else is upset.

But that's what I did.

I was twenty-eight. She was twenty-six. Our families had known each other forever, in that suffocating way where everyone's business is everyone's business and silence at the dinner table is interpreted as a wedding announcement.

We weren't in love. We weren't even particularly in like. We were two people who had been seated next to each other at enough functions that the adults in the room decided the universe was speaking, and the universe apparently sounded a lot like my Aunt Marlene.

Marlene cried when I hesitated. My mother went quiet, which was worse than crying. My father did the thing where he didn't look at me for a week, which in our family is the emotional equivalent of being excommunicated. Her side was doing the same dance over there. Two families running the same play from opposite sidelines.

I told myself a story. I told myself she was a good person — true. That we got along — sort of. That love grows — sometimes, not often, not for us. That I was being selfish for wanting more — the lie that does the most damage in any human life, by the way. The idea that wanting your own life is selfish.

I said yes the way you sign a lease on an apartment you've already half-moved into. The deposit was already gone. Walking away felt more expensive than staying.

It wasn't. Walking away is never more expensive than staying. I just didn't have the math yet.

The wedding was beautiful. Everyone said so. There's a photo of me at the reception holding a champagne glass and smiling, and I look at that photo now and I don't recognize the guy in it. He looks like an actor playing me. Which, in fairness, he was. He played me for the next twenty years.

I'm going to tell you the whole thing. Not because it's interesting — most disasters aren't — but because somebody out there right now is being pressured into something by people who love them, and love is the most expensive currency on earth when it's used as leverage.

— Lou
